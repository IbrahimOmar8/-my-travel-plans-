Luxor concentrates Egypt's greatest temple sites into a small area —
Karnak, the Valley of the Kings, Hatshepsut, the Colossi. The question
isn't whether to go, it's when. Here is how each month actually plays
out, based on running our own ground operations there.

## Peak season — October to March

This is when you'll see the daytime temperature sit comfortably in the
mid 20s°C, the Nile breeze is at its best, and Karnak feels civilised.
December and early January have the highest prices and the densest
groups — book at least four months ahead. February is our personal
favourite: warm enough for the cruise, cool enough at dawn to climb
into the Valley.

## Shoulder — April and September

April is hot but bearable. Crowds drop sharply after Easter, and so do
hotel rates. September is the same — humid by Red Sea standards but a
genuine bargain. Tombs that are unbearable at noon in summer are fine
if you start at 7 a.m.

## Off-peak — May to August

Daytime highs of 41–45°C. The plus side: you'll have the temples to
yourself in the late afternoon, and prices on Nile cruises can drop
30–40%. This is the right window if budget matters more than weather
and you don't mind splitting sightseeing across very early and very
late hours.

## A word on the Khamsin

Between March and May, expect 1–3 days of khamsin — hot, dusty winds
off the Sahara. Visibility for the Valley drops and hot-air balloons
won't fly. We always build in a buffer day around balloon mornings.

## What we book most often

If you have to pick one month and don't have constraints, we send most
guests in **early November** or **late February**. Crystal-clear light,
comfortable temperatures, balloons fly nearly every day, and you can
sit on the deck of a cruise without a jacket after sundown.
