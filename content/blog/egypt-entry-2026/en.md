Most of the questions we get in the days before a guest flies are not
about itineraries — they're about admin. Here is everything we send
them, in one place.

## Visa

The e-Visa portal at **visa2egypt.gov.eg** is the official route. It
costs USD 25 single-entry, USD 60 multiple. Approval takes 24–72 hours
in our experience. Most Western, GCC and Russian / Turkish passports
are eligible.

Visa-on-arrival is still available at Cairo, Hurghada and Sharm
airports for USD 25 cash, but in 2026 the queues have got noticeably
worse with the rise in arrivals. We always recommend the e-Visa.

If you're transiting Cairo for less than 24 hours and not leaving the
airside, you don't need a visa.

## eSIM and connectivity

Vodafone, Orange and Etisalat all sell tourist eSIMs at the airport.
**Vodafone has the best coverage at temple sites and on Nile cruise
stretches** — Karnak, Edfu, Kom Ombo. Plans start at around 350 EGP
for 10 GB / 14 days. Activate it before you clear customs.

For travellers without an eSIM-capable phone, Airalo and Holafly both
work fine and you can buy the data plan from home.

## Money

Egypt is mostly cash for small stuff: tips, taxis, mini-markets, temple
camera permits. Card works at restaurants, hotels and the GEM.

- Bring USD or EUR cash and exchange at a bank counter inside Cairo
  airport — the rate is comparable to in-town and you skip a stop.
- ATMs work for foreign cards but charge per withdrawal. Take out
  larger amounts less often.
- **Don't change money on the street.** The unofficial rate looks
  better but is illegal and frequently a scam.

## Tipping (baksheesh)

It is built into the system here. Budget around USD 8–12 per traveller
per day for guides, drivers, cruise crew combined. We give our guests
a tipping card on arrival with the breakdown by role.

## What to pack that nobody mentions

- A small torch — tomb interiors are darker than you think.
- A scarf or light long-sleeve for mosques, churches and the cold air
  on a felucca after sunset.
- Electrolyte sachets — Egypt's heat catches people out faster than
  the temperature alone suggests.
