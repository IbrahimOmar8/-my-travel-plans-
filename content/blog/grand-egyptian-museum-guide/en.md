After more than twenty years of construction, the Grand Egyptian Museum
(GEM) is finally fully open on the Giza plateau. It is the largest
archaeological museum in the world and it changes how you plan a first
trip to Egypt — for the better.

## Why it changes the itinerary

Until the GEM opened, the standard Cairo plan was: Egyptian Museum at
Tahrir on one day, Pyramids on another. Both were rushed and the old
Tahrir building, while charming, did the collection no favours.

The GEM brings the entire Tutankhamun collection — over 5,000 objects
shown together for the first time — into a purpose-built space within
walking distance of the Pyramids. It also displays the solar boats and
a 12-storey atrium that frames Khufu's pyramid through the window.

You can now do GEM in the morning and the plateau in the afternoon,
which is exactly the rhythm we recommend. Two and a half hours inside,
a quick lunch at the on-site café, and you're back at the Sphinx for
golden hour.

## What to prioritise inside

- **The grand staircase**: 80 statues arranged chronologically, from
  Old Kingdom to Ptolemaic. Walk up slowly.
- **Tutankhamun galleries**: book the timed-entry add-on at booking
  rather than queuing on the day.
- **Khufu boat hall**: the 4,500-year-old wooden boat, reassembled from
  1,224 pieces.
- **Children's museum**: if you're travelling with kids 6–12, this is
  worth 45 minutes alone.

## Logistics

- Ticket: 1,700 EGP for foreign adults including the Tutankhamun add-on
  (May 2026 prices).
- Cameras: phones allowed everywhere, DSLRs with a permit only.
- Plan 2.5–3 hours minimum. Real Egyptology fans plan a full day and
  re-enter after lunch.
- Combine with the Pyramids the same day — the entrance is a 5-minute
  drive away. We pre-book both for our guests and bundle the Egyptologist
  guide across the day.
